Essential Concepts

How The Web Works

Basically the internet is a bunch of computers that are connected together. When you sign on your personal computer and go to facebook, what is happening is your browser sends a requests to facebooks servers (specalized computers that store and transmit information) and facebook sends it back to you. This video explains it well.

HTML

HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language. This is the language browsers use to read webpages and deliver you your content. This website explains it well.

Tags and Elements

So the way I look at it is tages are like boxes and elements are what go into the boxes. "Tags contain elements which provide instructions for how information will be processed or displayed." "A basic unit of an HTML document. The name of the element is given in the name of the tag, and specifies the meaning associated with a block of text. Some elements are empty since they don't effect a block of text. Elements that have contents are also called containers, i.e., they contain attributes." Source.

Why Computers are stupid

Computers take things literally. They can't parse through natural language like humans can, and interpret ambiguous information correctly. You have to tell a computer to do everything. Every little automation it does on its own was programmed by a human. On their own without the most specific directions, they are useless pieces of metal and plastic. This is where programming langauges come in. They allow humans to get computers to do things.

Inline Vs. Block

From W3schools.com. A block-level element always starts on a new line and takes up the full width available (stretches out to the left and right as far as it can).

Inline elements don't start on a new line. The can be as wide as neccessary. div, a, span, and img are some examples of inline elements.

Document Object Model

Document Object Model or DOM is a "cross-platform and language-independent convention for representing and interacting with objects in HTML (and other markup languages). The nodes of every document are organized in a tree structure, called the DOM tree." This video explains it well.
